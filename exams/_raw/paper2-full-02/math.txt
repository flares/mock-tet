Q1 The HCF of 48 and 72 is:
A) 12
B) 24 *
C) 36
D) 48
E The common factors include 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, and 24; the greatest is 24.

Q2 Which number is divisible by 9?
A) 2345
B) 4212 *
C) 5003
D) 7012
E A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 9; 4+2+1+2=9.

Q3 The value of (-7) + 12 is:
A) -19
B) -5
C) 5 *
D) 19
E Moving 12 units right from -7 on the number line gives 5.

Q4 The decimal form of 3/8 is:
A) 0.125
B) 0.375 *
C) 0.625
D) 0.875
E Dividing 3 by 8 gives 0.375.

Q5 Which is an irrational number?
A) 0.75
B) 22/7
C) sqrt(2) *
D) -5
E sqrt(2) cannot be expressed as p/q for integers p and q with q not zero.

Q6 Using exponent laws, 2^3 x 2^4 equals:
A) 2^7 *
B) 2^12
C) 4^7
D) 8^4
E When bases are the same, exponents are added: 2^(3+4)=2^7.

Q7 If 5x - 3 = 17, then x equals:
A) 2
B) 3
C) 4 *
D) 5
E Add 3 to get 5x=20, then divide by 5 to get x=4.

Q8 The expansion of (a + b)^2 is:
A) a^2 + b^2
B) a^2 + 2ab + b^2 *
C) a^2 - 2ab + b^2
D) a^2 - b^2
E Squaring a binomial gives first square, twice the product, and second square.

Q9 A polynomial of degree 2 is called:
A) linear
B) quadratic *
C) cubic
D) constant
E A polynomial with highest power 2 is a quadratic polynomial.

Q10 If the discriminant of a quadratic equation is zero, its roots are:
A) real and equal *
B) real and unequal
C) non-real
D) always negative
E For ax^2+bx+c=0, D=0 means the two roots coincide.

Q11 The sum of angles in a triangle is:
A) 90 degrees
B) 180 degrees *
C) 270 degrees
D) 360 degrees
E The interior angles of every Euclidean triangle add up to 180 degrees.

Q12 Which congruence rule uses two sides and the included angle?
A) SSS
B) SAS *
C) ASA
D) RHS
E SAS requires two corresponding sides and the included angle to be equal.

Q13 The distance between (0,0) and (3,4) is:
A) 3
B) 4
C) 5 *
D) 7
E By the distance formula, sqrt(3^2+4^2)=5.

Q14 In a circle, a tangent at any point is perpendicular to the:
A) chord through the point
B) radius through the point *
C) diameter not passing through the point
D) arc
E The tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.

Q15 sin 30 degrees equals:
A) 0
B) 1/2 *
C) sqrt(3)/2
D) 1
E The standard trigonometric value of sin 30 degrees is 1/2.

Q16 The area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 6 cm is:
A) 16 cm2
B) 30 cm2 *
C) 60 cm2
D) 120 cm2
E Area of a triangle is 1/2 x base x height = 1/2 x 10 x 6 = 30 cm2.

Q17 The volume of a cuboid of length 5 cm, breadth 4 cm, and height 3 cm is:
A) 12 cm3
B) 20 cm3
C) 60 cm3 *
D) 80 cm3
E Volume of a cuboid is l x b x h = 5 x 4 x 3 = 60 cm3.

Q18 Heron's formula finds the area of a triangle when we know:
A) all three sides *
B) only one angle
C) only the perimeter of a circle
D) only the median
E Heron's formula uses the semiperimeter and the three side lengths.

Q19 The mean of 4, 6, 8, and 10 is:
A) 6
B) 7 *
C) 8
D) 9
E The sum is 28 and there are 4 values, so mean = 28/4 = 7.

Q20 The probability of getting a head when a fair coin is tossed once is:
A) 0
B) 1/4
C) 1/2 *
D) 1
E A fair coin has two equally likely outcomes, so P(head)=1/2.

Q21 A good mathematics classroom should treat errors as:
A) evidence for punishment
B) opportunities to understand thinking *
C) reasons to stop teaching
D) proof that the child cannot learn
E Errors reveal misconceptions and help the teacher plan diagnostic and remedial support.

Q22 The use of fraction strips mainly supports:
A) abstract memorisation only
B) concrete understanding of equivalent fractions *
C) speed copying
D) unrelated drawing
E Manipulatives like fraction strips make part-whole relationships visible.

Q23 The inductive method in mathematics moves from:
A) general rule to examples
B) examples to generalisation *
C) punishment to silence
D) symbols to no meaning
E Inductive teaching helps learners observe patterns and form rules from examples.

Q24 Diagnostic testing in mathematics helps identify:
A) the exact misconception behind errors *
B) only attendance
C) classroom decoration
D) sports ability
E Diagnostic tests locate specific conceptual gaps such as place value, operations, or algebraic reasoning.

Q25 Ethnomathematics means:
A) mathematics in cultural and community practices *
B) mathematics without numbers
C) only foreign mathematics
D) avoiding local examples
E Ethnomathematics connects mathematical ideas to practices in local culture, work, craft, and daily life.

Q26 A child who writes 1/3 > 1/2 may be overgeneralising:
A) larger denominator means larger fraction *
B) numerator has no role
C) fractions cannot be compared
D) both fractions are equal

Q27 Open-ended problems are useful because they:
A) allow multiple strategies and reasoning *
B) have no learning value
C) prevent discussion
D) require only one memorised line

Q28 In teaching geometry, dynamic software such as GeoGebra helps learners:
A) manipulate figures and observe invariants *

Q29 Performance-based assessment in mathematics may include:
A) explaining a solution with a model *
B) only copying a formula list
C) marking uniform colour
D) memorising roll numbers
E Performance tasks assess application, reasoning, representation, and communication.

Q30 Differentiated instruction in mathematics means:
A) changing support based on learners' needs *
B) giving no tasks to slow learners
C) teaching only the fastest group
D) using one worksheet forever
E Differentiation adjusts content, process, product, or support while keeping meaningful learning goals.
