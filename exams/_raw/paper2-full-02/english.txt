Q1 Read: "The village library opened its doors before sunrise, and children arrived carrying notebooks." What does this suggest?
A) The library was unpopular
B) The children were eager to learn *
C) The library opened only at night
D) The notebooks were being sold
E Early arrival with notebooks suggests enthusiasm and readiness for learning.

Q2 In the phrase "a fragile promise", the word fragile means:
A) strong
B) easily broken *
C) expensive
D) hidden
E Fragile means delicate or easily damaged, so a fragile promise may not be reliable.

Q3 The tone of a passage that praises community effort is most likely:
A) appreciative *
B) sarcastic
C) fearful
D) indifferent
E Praise for shared effort usually creates an appreciative tone.

Q4 An inference question asks the learner to:
A) copy a sentence exactly
B) read between the lines *
C) count punctuation marks
D) identify only the title
E Inference requires using textual clues and prior knowledge to understand implied meaning.

Q5 The main idea of a paragraph is:
A) the least important detail
B) the central point *
C) the longest word
D) the first punctuation mark
E The main idea is the central message that supporting details develop.

Q6 A synonym for "reluctant" is:
A) unwilling *
B) joyful
C) rapid
D) silent
E Reluctant means unwilling or hesitant to do something.

Q7 Which sentence uses the past perfect correctly?
A) She has finished before he arrived.
B) She had finished before he arrived. *
C) She finishes before he arrived.
D) She finishing before he arrived.
E Past perfect describes an action completed before another past action.

Q8 "The wind whispered through the trees" is an example of:
A) simile
B) personification *
C) hyperbole
D) alliteration only
E Personification gives a human action, whispering, to the wind.

Q9 A context clue is useful because it helps learners:
A) ignore unknown words
B) guess meaning from surrounding text *
C) skip comprehension
D) avoid reading
E Nearby words and sentences often provide clues to the meaning of an unfamiliar word.

Q10 A cohesive paragraph should have:
A) unrelated sentences
B) logical links between sentences *
C) only complex words
D) no topic sentence
E Cohesion comes from sequence, reference words, connectors, and a clear focus.

Q11 The communicative approach to language teaching emphasizes:
A) memorising grammar rules only
B) meaningful use of language in context *
C) translating every sentence
D) silent copying
E Communicative Language Teaching focuses on using language for real communication.

Q12 Krashen's Input Hypothesis suggests that learners benefit from input that is:
A) far below their level
B) slightly above their current level *
C) only in grammar tables
D) never meaningful
E Comprehensible input at i+1 is just beyond the learner's present competence.

Q13 Language acquisition differs from language learning because acquisition is:
A) natural and subconscious *
B) always written
C) based only on tests
D) unrelated to exposure
E Acquisition is a natural, meaningful, often subconscious process similar to first-language development.

Q15 A pre-reading activity should mainly:
A) activate prior knowledge *

Q16 Process writing includes:

Q17 Functional grammar is best taught by:

Q19 A portfolio in English assessment may include:

Q20 Remedial teaching begins with:
