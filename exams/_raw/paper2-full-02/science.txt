Q1 Which nutrient is mainly needed for body-building and repair?
A) Carbohydrate
B) Protein *
C) Water
D) Roughage
E Proteins support growth and repair of body tissues.

Q2 Deficiency of vitamin C causes:
A) rickets
B) scurvy *
C) night blindness
D) goitre
E Scurvy is caused by vitamin C deficiency and may lead to bleeding gums and weakness.

Q3 The process used to separate grain from chaff by wind is:
A) filtration
B) winnowing *
C) distillation
D) chromatography
E Winnowing separates heavier grains from lighter chaff using moving air.

Q4 Which fibre is synthetic?
A) Cotton
B) Wool
C) Jute
D) Nylon *
E Nylon is a man-made synthetic fibre, unlike cotton, wool, and jute.

Q5 Litmus turns red in:
A) acidic solution *
B) basic solution
C) neutral salt only
D) pure oil
E Blue litmus turns red in acidic solutions.

Q6 A thermosetting plastic is:
A) polythene
B) PVC
C) bakelite *
D) nylon thread
E Bakelite hardens permanently on heating and cannot be remoulded.

Q7 The atomic number of an element equals the number of:
A) neutrons
B) protons *
C) molecules
D) shells only
E Atomic number is defined by the number of protons in the nucleus.

Q8 Isotopes of an element have:
A) same atomic number but different mass number *
B) different atomic number but same mass number
C) no protons
D) identical neutron number always
E Isotopes have the same protons but different neutrons, so their mass numbers differ.

Q9 The functional group in ethanol is:
A) -COOH
B) -OH *
C) -CHO
D) -CO-
E Ethanol is an alcohol and contains the hydroxyl group, -OH.

Q10 The basic structural and functional unit of life is:
A) tissue
B) cell *
C) organ
D) organ system
E Cells are the smallest living units capable of carrying out life processes.

Q11 Mitochondria are known as the powerhouse of the cell because they:
A) store hereditary material
B) release energy through respiration *
C) make proteins only
D) give green colour
E Mitochondria produce ATP during cellular respiration.

Q12 Xylem mainly transports:
A) prepared food
B) water and minerals *
C) hormones only
D) carbon dioxide
E Xylem carries water and dissolved minerals from roots to other parts of the plant.

Q13 Binary fission is a form of:
A) asexual reproduction *
B) sexual reproduction
C) pollination
D) seed dispersal
E In binary fission one parent cell divides into two daughter cells without gametes.

Q14 In a food chain, producers are usually:
A) green plants *
B) carnivores
C) decomposers only
D) parasites only
E Green plants produce food by photosynthesis and form the first trophic level.

Q15 A reflex arc includes:
A) receptor, sensory neuron, spinal cord, motor neuron, effector *
B) only heart and lungs
C) only endocrine glands
D) only bones
E A reflex arc is the neural pathway for a quick automatic response.

Q16 The SI unit of force is:
A) joule
B) watt
C) newton *
D) pascal-hour
E Force is measured in newtons in the SI system.

Q17 Newton's first law is also called the law of:
A) inertia *
B) gravitation
C) reflection
D) conservation of charge
E The first law states that a body resists change in its state of motion, which is inertia.

Q18 Ohm's law states:
A) V = IR *
B) P = VI only
C) F = ma
D) E = mc2
E Ohm's law relates potential difference, current, and resistance as V=IR.

Q19 A convex lens is used to correct:
A) myopia with diverging lens
B) hypermetropia *
C) colour blindness
D) night blindness
E Hypermetropia is corrected using a converging, or convex, lens.

Q20 The gas mainly responsible for ozone layer depletion is:
A) oxygen
B) nitrogen
C) chlorofluorocarbon *
D) helium
E CFCs release chlorine radicals that destroy ozone molecules in the stratosphere.

Q21 Inquiry-based science teaching begins with:
A) a question or problem to investigate *
B) copying definitions only
C) banning observation
D) final marks first
E Inquiry learning starts from curiosity, questions, observation, evidence, and explanation.

Q22 A good science experiment should have:
A) uncontrolled variables only
B) a fair test with controlled variables *
C) no observation
D) no record of data
E Controlling variables helps test the effect of one factor reliably.

Q23 Concept cartoons are useful because they:
A) expose misconceptions for discussion *
B) replace all experiments
C) prevent questioning
D) give only final answers
E Concept cartoons present alternative ideas and help students debate evidence and misconceptions.

Q24 A common misconception about plants is that they:
A) respire only at night *
B) need water
C) contain cells
D) grow from seeds
E Plants respire all the time, though photosynthesis occurs only in light.

Q25 Laboratory work should be assessed by:
A) only final written marks
B) observation of skills, safety, records, and reasoning *
C) neat uniform only
D) speed alone
E Practical assessment includes process skills, safe handling, data recording, and interpretation.

Q26 A model of the human heart is useful because it:
A) makes an invisible internal structure easier to visualise *
B) proves all details perfectly
C) removes the need for explanation
D) prevents questions
E Models help students visualise structures and relationships, though their limits should be discussed.

Q27 Environmental education in science should promote:
A) awareness, responsibility, and sustainable action *
B) memorisation without action
C) ignoring local problems
D) wasting resources
E Science education connects environmental concepts with responsible decisions and sustainable practices.

Q28 An exit ticket is an example of:
A) formative assessment *
B) annual certification only
C) punishment
D) laboratory equipment
E Exit tickets quickly reveal what students understood at the end of a lesson.

Q29 Teaching weight and mass should clarify that:
A) mass and weight are identical everywhere
B) mass is amount of matter, weight is gravitational force *
C) weight has no unit
D) mass changes with location like weight
E Mass measures matter, while weight depends on gravitational force acting on that mass.

Q30 STEM integration encourages students to:
A) connect science, technology, engineering, and mathematics in problem solving *
B) study each idea without application
C) avoid design tasks
D) memorise only definitions
E STEM tasks integrate concepts and practices across disciplines to solve real problems.
