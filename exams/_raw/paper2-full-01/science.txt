Q1 Which of the following is a macronutrient required by the human body?
A) Vitamin A
B) Iodine
C) Carbohydrates *
D) Zinc
E Macronutrients (carbohydrates, proteins, and fats) are required in large quantities, while micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) are needed in smaller amounts.

Q2 What is the primary function of mitochondria in a cell?
A) Photosynthesis
B) Protein synthesis
C) Energy production *
D) Storage of water
E Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell, producing ATP through aerobic respiration to provide energy for cellular activities.

Q3 Which gas is absorbed by plants during photosynthesis?
A) Oxygen
B) Carbon dioxide *
C) Nitrogen
D) Hydrogen
E Plants use carbon dioxide from the atmosphere in the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle) to synthesise glucose.

Q4 What is the correct order of the food chain: grass → ? → eagle
A) Frog
B) Mouse *
C) Flower
D) Snake
E In this food chain, the mouse (primary consumer) feeds on grass and is then consumed by the eagle (secondary consumer).

Q5 Which of the following is a non-metal that conducts electricity?
A) Carbon (graphite) *
B) Copper
C) Aluminium
D) Iron
E Graphite, an allotrope of carbon, conducts electricity due to the presence of free electrons in its structure.

Q6 The pH value of a neutral solution is:
A) 0
B) 7 *
C) 14
D) 10
E The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral; values below 7 indicate acidity and above 7 indicate alkalinity.

Q7 Which hormone controls the growth and development during adolescence?
A) Insulin
B) Testosterone and Oestrogen *
C) Adrenaline
D) Thyroxine
E Sex hormones (testosterone in males and oestrogen in females) are responsible for secondary sexual characteristics and growth during puberty.

Q8 What is the main component of bone that provides strength?
A) Calcium phosphate *
B) Collagen
C) Fat
D) Water
E Calcium phosphate gives bones their hardness and rigidity, while collagen provides flexibility.

Q9 Which process describes the movement of water from roots to leaves in plants?
A) Transpiration
B) Photosynthesis
C) Translocation
D) Osmosis *
E Osmosis allows water to move into root cells due to a water potential gradient, and capillary action and root pressure push water upward through the xylem.

Q10 A student observes that plants grown in the dark do not produce starch. Which scientific method step is this?
A) Hypothesis
B) Observation *
C) Experimentation
D) Conclusion
E Observation is the first step in the scientific method where one notices a phenomenon or outcome without yet drawing conclusions.

Q11 Which of the following is an example of asexual reproduction?
A) Pollination
B) Budding in yeast *
C) Fertilisation
D) Meiosis
E Budding produces identical offspring from a single parent organism without the fusion of gametes, making it asexual reproduction.

Q12 The SI unit of force is:
A) Newton *
B) Joule
C) Watt
D) Pascal
E Newton (N) is the standard unit of force in the International System of Units, defined as the force needed to accelerate 1 kg by 1 m/s².

Q13 Which vitamin is synthesised by the human body when skin is exposed to sunlight?
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin B
C) Vitamin C
D) Vitamin D *
E Vitamin D is synthesised in the skin through the action of ultraviolet (UV) rays from sunlight on 7-dehydrocholesterol.

Q14 A deficiency of which mineral causes goitre in humans?
A) Iron
B) Iodine *
C) Copper
D) Zinc
E Iodine is essential for thyroid hormone synthesis; its deficiency leads to thyroid enlargement (goitre) and metabolic disorders.

Q15 Which of the following best describes the concept of biodiversity?
A) The number of plants in a forest
B) The variety of species, genes, and ecosystems in a region *
C) The total biomass of organisms
D) The adaptation of a single species
E Biodiversity encompasses all levels of biological variation: genetic diversity within species, species diversity, and ecosystem diversity.

Q16 What is the primary role of the pancreas in the digestive system?
A) Storage of digestive enzymes
B) Production of bile
C) Secretion of digestive enzymes and hormones *
D) Absorption of nutrients
E The pancreas produces digestive enzymes (amylase, protease, lipase) and hormones (insulin, glucagon) that regulate digestion and glucose metabolism.

Q17 Which of the following is a misconception about photosynthesis?
A) It occurs only during the day
B) It requires chlorophyll
C) It produces glucose and oxygen
D) It requires heat energy but not light *
E Photosynthesis requires light energy directly in light-dependent reactions; heat alone cannot substitute for light in this process.

Q18 The process by which water vapour is lost from plant leaves is called:
A) Evaporation
B) Transpiration *
C) Precipitation
D) Condensation
E Transpiration is the loss of water vapour from plant leaves (primarily through stomata) as part of the water cycle in plants.

Q19 Which structure in a plant cell is responsible for storing starch?
A) Chloroplast
B) Mitochondrion
C) Amyloplast *
D) Vacuole
E Amyloplasts are specialised plastids that synthesise and store starch in plant cells, particularly in roots and seeds.

Q20 When teaching the concept of "weight vs mass", which misconception most commonly arises?
A) Students think weight is constant everywhere
B) Students confuse weight (force) with mass (quantity of matter) *
C) Students think mass increases with gravity
D) Students overestimate their own mass
E A common error is treating weight and mass as identical; weight varies with gravity (W = mg), but mass is invariant.

Q21 Which of the following is a characteristic of living organisms that distinguishes them from non-living things?
A) Reproduction *
B) Hardness
C) Weight
D) Colour
E All living organisms can reproduce (asexually or sexually) to produce offspring, a key characteristic absent in non-living matter.

Q22 The phenomenon by which the frequency of a sound wave changes due to the motion of the source is called:
A) Refraction
B) Diffraction
C) Doppler effect *
D) Interference
E The Doppler effect explains why the pitch of a siren changes as an ambulance moves towards or away from an observer.

Q23 Which of the following is the correct electron configuration of an oxygen atom (atomic number 8)?
A) 2, 5
B) 2, 6 *
C) 2, 4
D) 2, 8
E Oxygen has 8 electrons: 2 in the first shell (K) and 6 in the second shell (L), giving the configuration 2,6.

Q24 A pendulum swings faster at lower temperatures. Which step of the scientific method should a student use to verify this observation?
A) Hypothesis
B) Experimentation and controlling variables *
C) Literature review
D) Observation only
E To verify an observation, a controlled experiment testing the hypothesis with proper variables is essential; this requires controlling factors like pendulum length.

Q25 Which process results in the formation of gametes in humans?
A) Mitosis
B) Meiosis *
C) Binary fission
D) Budding
E Meiosis produces haploid gametes (sperm and ova) with half the chromosome number (23) of somatic cells (46), essential for sexual reproduction.

Q26 In a food web, organisms at the highest trophic level are called:
A) Producers
B) Primary consumers
C) Secondary consumers
D) Top predators *
E Top predators occupy the apex of food chains and are not preyed upon by other organisms in the ecosystem.

Q27 Which of the following is an example of a simple machine that provides mechanical advantage?
A) Pulley *
B) Magnet
C) Thermometer
D) Compass
E A pulley is a simple machine that changes the direction of force and can provide mechanical advantage, making lifting easier.

Q28 The respiratory system's primary function is to:
A) Produce energy
B) Transport oxygen through the body
C) Exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide with the environment *
D) Filter blood
E The respiratory system (lungs and airways) facilitates the exchange of O₂ and CO₂ between the air and blood through diffusion in alveoli.

Q29 When teaching the concept of "burning" to upper primary students, which is the best approach?
A) Memorise the chemical formula of combustion
B) Conduct a controlled observation: light a candle, observe the flame, allow cooling, and discuss oxygen's role *
C) Provide a definition without demonstration
D) Show only photos of fire
E An inquiry-based, observational approach with safe experimentation allows students to construct understanding of combustion empirically.

Q30 Which of the following is an example of a renewable natural resource?
A) Coal
B) Petroleum
C) Forest wood *
D) Iron ore
E Forests are renewable if sustainably managed—trees can regrow, unlike fossil fuels (coal, petroleum) or limited minerals that take millions of years to form.
