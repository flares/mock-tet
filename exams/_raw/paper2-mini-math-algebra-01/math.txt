Q1 Which of the following pairs consists of like terms?
A) 3x²y and 5xy²
B) 7ab and -2ba *
C) 4p²q and 4pq²
D) 9mn and 9m
E Like terms have identical variable parts with the same exponents; 7ab and -2ba both have variables a and b to the first power, so they are like terms.

Q2 The coefficient of x in the expression 5 - 7x + 3x²y - 2xy is:
A) -7
B) 3
C) -7 - 2y *
D) -9
E Collecting all terms containing x to the first power: -7x - 2xy = (-7 - 2y)x, so the coefficient of x is -7 - 2y.

Q3 Simplify: (4a² - 3a + 7) - (2a² + 5a - 4)
A) 2a² - 8a + 11 *
B) 2a² + 2a + 3
C) 6a² - 8a + 11
D) 2a² - 8a + 3
E Subtract by changing signs: 4a² - 3a + 7 - 2a² - 5a + 4 = 2a² - 8a + 11.

Q4 The product (2x - 3)(x + 4) equals:
A) 2x² + 5x - 12 *
B) 2x² - 5x - 12
C) 2x² + 11x - 12
D) 2x² + 5x + 12
E Using FOIL: 2x·x + 2x·4 - 3·x - 3·4 = 2x² + 8x - 3x - 12 = 2x² + 5x - 12.

Q5 Divide 12x³y² - 8x²y³ + 4x²y² by 4x²y²:
A) 3x - 2y + 1 *
B) 3xy - 2y + 1
C) 3x - 2y² + 1
D) 3x - 2y + 4
E Dividing each term: 12x³y²/4x²y² = 3x, -8x²y³/4x²y² = -2y, 4x²y²/4x²y² = 1.

Q6 If (a + b)² = 49 and ab = 10, then a² + b² equals:
A) 29 *
B) 39
C) 59
D) 69
E Since (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b², we have 49 = a² + b² + 20, so a² + b² = 29.

Q7 Expand: (3x - 2y)²
A) 9x² - 12xy + 4y² *
B) 9x² + 12xy + 4y²
C) 9x² - 6xy + 4y²
D) 3x² - 12xy + 2y²
E Using (a-b)² = a² - 2ab + b²: (3x)² - 2(3x)(2y) + (2y)² = 9x² - 12xy + 4y².

Q8 The value of 103 × 97 using identities is:
A) 9991 *
B) 9999
C) 9981
D) 10001
E Writing as (100+3)(100-3) = 100² - 3² = 10000 - 9 = 9991.

Q9 If a + b + c = 10 and a² + b² + c² = 40, then ab + bc + ca equals:
A) 30 *
B) 20
C) 60
D) 50
E Using (a+b+c)² = a² + b² + c² + 2(ab+bc+ca): 100 = 40 + 2(ab+bc+ca), so ab+bc+ca = 30.

Q10 Factorise: 12x²y - 18xy² + 6xy
A) 6xy(2x - 3y + 1) *
B) 6xy(2x - 3y)
C) 6x(2xy - 3y² + 1)
D) 6xy(2x + 3y + 1)
E The HCF of the three terms is 6xy; dividing each gives 2x, -3y, and 1.

Q11 Factorise: x² - 16y²
A) (x - 4y)(x + 4y) *
B) (x - 8y)(x + 8y)
C) (x - 4y)²
D) (x + 4y)²
E This is a difference of squares: a² - b² = (a-b)(a+b) with a = x and b = 4y.

Q12 Factorise: x² + 7x + 12
A) (x + 3)(x + 4) *
B) (x + 2)(x + 6)
C) (x - 3)(x - 4)
D) (x + 1)(x + 12)
E We need two numbers whose product is 12 and sum is 7: these are 3 and 4.

Q13 Factorise by grouping: ax + ay + bx + by
A) (a + b)(x + y) *
B) (a - b)(x + y)
C) (a + b)(x - y)
D) (ab + xy)
E Group: a(x+y) + b(x+y) = (a+b)(x+y).

Q14 If 5(x - 3) = 3(x + 5), then x equals:
A) 15 *
B) 5
C) 10
D) -15
E Expanding: 5x - 15 = 3x + 15, so 2x = 30, giving x = 15.

Q15 The sum of three consecutive integers is 78. The largest of the three is:
A) 27 *
B) 25
C) 26
D) 28
E Let the integers be n-1, n, n+1; their sum is 3n = 78, so n = 26 and the largest is 27.

Q16 A father is four times as old as his son. After 5 years, the father will be three times as old. The son's present age (in years) is:
A) 10 *
B) 8
C) 12
D) 15
E Let son's age be x; father is 4x. Then 4x + 5 = 3(x + 5) gives 4x + 5 = 3x + 15, so x = 10.

Q17 Solve for x: (x + 2)/3 - (x - 1)/4 = 2
A) 13 *
B) 17
C) 11
D) 9
E Multiply through by 12: 4(x+2) - 3(x-1) = 24, giving 4x + 8 - 3x + 3 = 24, so x + 11 = 24 and x = 13.

Q18 The solution of the system x + y = 7 and x - y = 3 is:
A) x = 5, y = 2 *
B) x = 4, y = 3
C) x = 6, y = 1
D) x = 3, y = 4
E Adding the two equations: 2x = 10, so x = 5; then y = 7 - 5 = 2.

Q19 If 2x + 3y = 12 and x = 3, then y equals:
A) 2 *
B) 3
C) 4
D) 6
E Substituting x = 3: 6 + 3y = 12, so 3y = 6 and y = 2.

Q20 Solve by elimination: 3x + 2y = 16, 5x - 2y = 8. The value of x is:
A) 3 *
B) 4
C) 2
D) 5
E Adding the two equations eliminates y: 8x = 24, so x = 3.

Q21 If a : b = 2 : 3 and b : c = 4 : 5, then a : c is:
A) 8 : 15 *
B) 2 : 5
C) 5 : 8
D) 10 : 12
E Make b common: a:b = 8:12 and b:c = 12:15, so a:c = 8:15.

Q22 If y varies inversely as x and y = 8 when x = 6, then y when x = 12 is:
A) 4 *
B) 16
C) 8
D) 2
E In inverse variation, xy = k, so k = 48; when x = 12, y = 48/12 = 4.

Q23 The degree of the polynomial 5x³y² - 4x²y + 7xy⁴ + 2 is:
A) 5 *
B) 4
C) 3
D) 6
E Degree of each term: 5, 3, 5, 0; the highest is 5 (from 5x³y² and 7xy⁴).

Q24 The zero of the polynomial p(x) = 3x - 12 is:
A) 4 *
B) -4
C) 3
D) -3
E Setting p(x) = 0: 3x - 12 = 0, so x = 4.

Q25 The remainder when x³ - 2x² + x + 1 is divided by (x - 1) is:
A) 1 *
B) 0
C) 2
D) -1
E By the remainder theorem, the remainder equals p(1) = 1 - 2 + 1 + 1 = 1.

Q26 If (x - 2) is a factor of x³ - 3x² + kx - 4, the value of k is:
A) 4 *
B) -4
C) 2
D) 8
E By the factor theorem, p(2) = 0: 8 - 12 + 2k - 4 = 0, so 2k = 8 and k = 4.

Q27 The discriminant of the quadratic 2x² - 5x + 3 is:
A) 1 *
B) 49
C) -1
D) 25
E Discriminant = b² - 4ac = 25 - 24 = 1.

Q28 If the roots of x² - px + 12 = 0 are 3 and 4, the value of p is:
A) 7 *
B) -7
C) 12
D) 1
E Sum of roots = p = 3 + 4 = 7 (and product 3 × 4 = 12 confirms).

Q29 The 15th term of the AP 7, 10, 13, 16, ... is:
A) 49 *
B) 52
C) 46
D) 45
E nth term = a + (n-1)d = 7 + 14(3) = 7 + 42 = 49.

Q30 The sum of the first 20 terms of the AP 2, 5, 8, 11, ... is:
A) 610 *
B) 580
C) 600
D) 620
E Sum = (n/2)[2a + (n-1)d] = 10[4 + 19·3] = 10[4 + 57] = 10 × 61 = 610.
