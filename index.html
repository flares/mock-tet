Full syllabus for CTET Paper 2 (Telugu-medium adaptation) covering Classes VI–VIII with extended topics from Classes IX–X. View the official PDF below or click any section to expand detailed topics.

Child Development & Pedagogy (CDP) 30 Questions · 30 Marks ▼
Child Development — 15 Questions
  • Concept of development; growth vs development; principles of development
  • Influence of heredity and environment on development
  • Stages of development: infancy, childhood, adolescence — physical, cognitive, emotional, social, moral dimensions
  • Piaget's cognitive development theory — 4 stages; schema, assimilation, accommodation, equilibration
  • Vygotsky's socio-cultural theory; Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD); scaffolding
  • Kohlberg's moral development theory — 6 stages, 3 levels
  • Howard Gardner's Multiple Intelligences — 8 intelligences
  • Erikson's psychosocial stages
  • Language development; Chomsky's Language Acquisition Device (LAD)
  • Theories of intelligence — Spearman's g factor, Thurstone's primary mental abilities
  • Emotional intelligence; social-emotional learning
  • Gender as a social construct; gender roles; gender-biased education
  • Individual differences and diversity in learning styles
  • Adolescence: characteristics, identity formation, peer influence, problems
Inclusive Education — 5 Questions
  • Concepts of diversity, disability, and inclusion
  • Types of disabilities: intellectual, learning disability (dyslexia, dyscalculia, dysgraphia), ADHD, hearing impairment, visual impairment, autism spectrum disorder, cerebral palsy
  • Inclusive classroom strategies; Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
  • Rights of children with disabilities; RTE Act 2009; Disabilities Act
  • Role of the teacher in an inclusive classroom
Learning & Pedagogy — 10 Questions
  • How children think and learn; why children fail to achieve success
  • Behaviourism — Pavlov (classical conditioning), Skinner (operant conditioning)
  • Constructivism — Bruner, Piaget; Social Learning Theory — Bandura
  • Motivation: intrinsic vs extrinsic; Maslow's hierarchy of needs
  • Factors affecting learning: attention, memory, perception, emotion
  • Formative vs summative assessment; Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE)
  • Critical thinking, problem-solving, creative thinking
  • Child-centred education; activity-based learning; collaborative learning
  • Role of language and cognition in learning
  • NCF 2005 key recommendations; NEP 2020 aspects for upper primary
English 30 Questions · 30 Marks ▼
Reading Comprehension — 15 Questions
  • Unseen passages: narrative, discursive, and literary texts
  • Inference, vocabulary in context, tone, main idea, and detail questions
Pedagogy of Language Development — 15 Questions
  • Language acquisition vs language learning; Krashen's Monitor Model and Input Hypothesis
  • LSRW (listening, speaking, reading, writing) in second-language development
  • Methods: Communicative Language Teaching (CLT), Direct Method, Grammar-Translation, Audio-Lingual
  • Reading: decoding, fluency, comprehension strategies
  • Writing: process writing, drafting, revising, creative writing
  • Functional grammar in context (not prescriptive rules)
  • Role of L1 (Telugu) in learning L2 (English); multilingual classroom challenges
  • Formative evaluation tools: portfolio, observation, peer assessment
  • Remedial teaching in English
Telugu 30 Questions · 30 Marks · Questions in Telugu Script ▼
Reading Comprehension — 15 Questions
  • Telugu passages (narrative, poetic, informational) for upper-primary readers
  • Comprehension, inference, vocabulary-in-context, tone
Pedagogy of Telugu Language — 15 Questions
  • Mother-tongue acquisition principles; importance of L1 in early education
  • LSRW in Telugu; oral fluency before literacy
  • Teaching reading and writing: phonics, whole-language, balanced approach
  • Telugu script literacy: vowels, consonants, conjuncts; common reading errors
  • Literature, folklore, and oral tradition in Telugu pedagogy
  • Functional grammar: sandhi, samaasa, vibhakti basics
  • Multilingual classroom — relating Telugu to English / Hindi / Sanskrit
  • Formative evaluation tools; remedial teaching for struggling readers
Mathematics 30 Questions · 30 Marks · ≈20 Content + ≈10 Pedagogy ▼
Number System
  • Integers: positive/negative, absolute value, number line; divisibility rules; LCM and HCF (Euclid's algorithm)
  • Fractions (types, operations); rational numbers (definition, representation, operations)
  • Decimals: terminating and non-terminating; conversion between fractions and decimals
  • Exponents and powers; laws of exponents; scientific notation
  • Class 9–10: Real numbers, Euclid's division lemma, irrational numbers (√2, √3 proofs), decimal expansion of rationals
Algebra
  • Variables, constants, algebraic expressions; ratio and proportion; unitary method; direct and inverse proportion
  • Algebraic identities: (a±b)², (a+b)(a−b), (a+b+c)², (a±b)³, a³±b³
  • Factorisation: common factor, grouping, difference of squares, trinomials
  • Linear equations in one and two variables; graphical and algebraic solutions
  • Class 9–10: Polynomials (remainder and factor theorems); quadratic equations (discriminant, nature of roots, quadratic formula); Arithmetic Progressions (nth term, sum formula)
Geometry
  • Lines, angles (types), parallel lines and transversal
  • Triangles: angle-sum, exterior angle, triangle inequality; congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS, AAS); similarity (AA, SAS, SSS)
  • Quadrilaterals: types and properties; angle sum; polygons — interior/exterior angle formulae
  • Circles: chord, arc, sector, segment; central and inscribed angles; tangent properties
  • Symmetry: line and rotational symmetry; 3D shapes — nets; Euler's formula (V + F = E + 2)
  • Constructions: bisectors, triangles, quadrilaterals
  • Class 9–10: Coordinate geometry (distance, section formula, mid-point, slope, collinearity); Trigonometry (sin/cos/tan, identities, complementary angles, heights and distances)
Mensuration
  • Perimeter and area: square, rectangle, parallelogram, triangle, trapezium, rhombus, circle; combined shapes; paths
  • Surface area and volume: cube, cuboid, cylinder, cone, sphere, hemisphere, frustum
  • Class 9–10: Heron's formula; complex 3D figures; conversion of solids
Data Handling
  • Bar graphs, histograms, pie charts, line graphs; frequency distribution tables
  • Mean (direct, assumed mean, step deviation), median, mode
  • Probability: experimental vs theoretical; complementary events
  • Class 9–10: Cumulative frequency (ogive); quartiles; addition rule of probability
Pedagogical Issues in Mathematics
  • Nature of mathematics: abstract, logical, hierarchical structure; role of conjecture and proof
  • Language of mathematics; community mathematics; ethnomathematics
  • Common student misconceptions in arithmetic, algebra, and geometry
  • Strategies: constructivist approach, inductive-deductive methods, problem-solving, project method
  • Manipulatives and technology: Geogebra, abacus, Dienes blocks, number strips
  • Evaluation: oral, written, performance-based, diagnostic testing; remedial teaching
  • Differentiated instruction for diverse learners
Science 30 Questions · 30 Marks · ≈20 Content + ≈10 Pedagogy ▼
Food & Materials
  • Sources of food; components: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins (A, B-complex, C, D), minerals; deficiency diseases
  • Separation methods: hand-picking, winnowing, sieving, sedimentation, filtration, evaporation, distillation, chromatography
  • Fibre to Fabric: natural (cotton, wool, silk, jute) and synthetic fibres (nylon, polyester, acrylic)
  • Metals and Non-metals: properties, reactivity series
  • Acids, Bases and Salts: indicators, pH scale, neutralisation; everyday applications
  • Class 9–10: Atoms & Molecules (laws of chemical combination, Dalton's theory, molar mass, Avogadro's number); Atomic structure (Thomson, Rutherford, Bohr models; atomic number; isotopes); Carbon compounds (covalent bonding, homologous series, IUPAC naming, ethanol, ethanoic acid, soaps and detergents); Periodic table (Dobereiner, Newlands, Mendeleev, Modern periodic law and trends)
Living World
  • Cell: structure (plant and animal); organelles and functions; prokaryotic vs eukaryotic
  • Organisation: cell → tissue → organ → organ system; animal and plant tissue types
  • Microorganisms: bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa; useful and harmful; disease and immunity; food preservation
  • Human body: digestive, respiratory, circulatory, excretory, skeletal and muscular systems
  • Reproduction: asexual (binary fission, budding, spore formation, vegetative propagation); sexual reproduction in plants and humans; adolescence and puberty
  • Ecosystems: food chains and webs, trophic levels, energy flow, biotic and abiotic factors; diversity and classification
  • Class 9–10: Life Processes (photosynthesis, aerobic/anaerobic respiration, transportation, excretion — nephron); Control & Coordination (neurons, reflex arc, CNS/PNS, endocrine system); Heredity & Evolution (Mendel's laws, monohybrid/dihybrid cross, natural selection, speciation); Our Environment (biodegradable vs non-biodegradable, ozone layer, greenhouse effect, waste management)
Force, Motion & Energy
  • Motion: types, speed, velocity, acceleration; distance-time and velocity-time graphs
  • Force: contact and non-contact; friction (types, factors, useful/harmful)
  • Simple machines: lever (3 classes), pulley, wheel and axle, inclined plane; mechanical advantage
  • Work, energy, power; KE and PE; conservation of mechanical energy
  • Sound: production, propagation, frequency, amplitude; echo; noise pollution
  • Light: rectilinear propagation; reflection (plane and curved mirrors); refraction (Snell's law, total internal reflection); dispersion; lenses; human eye and defects (myopia, hypermetropia)
  • Class 9–10: Newton's 3 laws; gravitation (universal law, g, buoyancy, Archimedes' principle); Electricity (Ohm's law, series/parallel circuits, power, heating effect, domestic circuits); Magnetic effects (Oersted, electromagnet, Fleming's rules, electric motor and generator, AC vs DC)
Natural Phenomena & Resources
  • Water cycle; types of rainfall; thunder and lightning; earthquakes (seismic waves, Richter scale)
  • Air: composition, oxygen and nitrogen cycles, air pollution
  • Water: importance, water cycle, pollution, conservation, rainwater harvesting
  • Soil: formation, layers, soil types, erosion, conservation
  • Coal and petroleum: formation, fractions of petroleum; fossil fuels as non-renewable
  • Forests: importance, deforestation, conservation; 3Rs; wildlife protection; biosphere reserves
  • Class 9–10: Ecosystem in detail; ozone depletion; management of natural resources (Chipko movement, dam controversy, sustainability)
Pedagogical Issues in Science
  • Nature and structure of sciences: scientific method, hypothesis, experimentation, objectivity, falsifiability
  • Aims of science education: scientific temper, curiosity, environmental awareness
  • Approaches: inductive, deductive, inquiry-based, project-based, integrated
  • Importance of observation and experimentation; lab skills; designing experiments
  • Concept mapping; analogical reasoning; use of models, simulations, ICT tools
  • Common misconceptions (e.g. weight vs mass, photosynthesis at night, evolution as progress)
  • Evaluation: lab work assessment, MCQs vs open-ended questions, rubrics; formative strategies (think-pair-share, exit tickets, concept cartoons)
  • Remedial teaching; STEM integration; gender equity in science
